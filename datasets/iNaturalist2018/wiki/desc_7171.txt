Linaria dalmatica is a herbaceous plant native to western Asia and southeastern Europe that has become a weed in other areas. Its common names include Balkan toadflax, broadleaf toadflax, and Dalmatian toadflax.


== Invasive species ==
Linaria dalmatica is listed as a noxious weed in 12 U.S. states. It is a grassland invader native to the Mediterranean region, and it was introduced to North America in the late 1800s.  It has fast-growing strong, horizontal roots, and can withstand cold, and is a problem for farms and grasslands in the interior of British Columbia.
