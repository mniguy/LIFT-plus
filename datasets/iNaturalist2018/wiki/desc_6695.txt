Baptisia alba, commonly called white wild indigo or  white false indigo, is a herbaceous plant in the bean family Fabaceae. It is native in central and eastern North America.'There are two varieties, Baptisia alba var. alba and Baptisia alba var. macrophylla.


== Description ==
Leaves have alternate arrangement, and are trifoliate. White flowers occur from a long spike inflorescence. Flower blooms occur between April to July, earlier in the southern part of the range. The species is native to grasslands, but is grown in some gardens. 
Baptisia alba is described as a facultative upland plant in all parts of its range.
