Furcula borealis, the white furcula moth, is a moth of the  family Notodontidae. It is found from New Hampshire to Texas and Florida, as well as in Colorado and South Dakota.The wingspan is 31â€“42 mm. Adults are on wing from April to August.
The larvae feed on Prunus avium, Salix and Populus species.


== Taxonomy ==
The species has been treated as a subspecies of the Furcula bicuspis, but has been elevated back to species status.
