Chlosyne gabbii, or Gabb's checkerspot, is a butterfly from the family Nymphalidae.


== Subspecies ==
Chlosyne gabbii gabbii
Chlosyne gabbii atrifasciata Emmel & Mattoon, 1998


== Description ==
Chlosyne gabbii has a wingspan of about 32–45 millimetres (1.3–1.8 in). The upperside of the wings is checkered with bright orange-brown and black, while the underside of hindwing shows pearly-white spots. Females are lighter than the males. Adults can be encountered from May to July.Larvae feed  on Corethrogyne filaginifolia, Heterotheca grandiflora and Haplopappus squarrosus.


== Distribution ==
This very rare species can be found in California in the western United States. It is threatened throughout its range.
