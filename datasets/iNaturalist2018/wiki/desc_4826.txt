Zonitoides arboreus, commonly known as the quick gloss snail, is species of small air-breathing land snail, a terrestrial pulmonate gastropod mollusk in the family Gastrodontidae.

== Description ==
The shell is pale yellowish brown, translucent, faintly and irregularly striated. The shell has 4-4.5 whorls. The shell is sometimes very slightly keeled. The umbilicus is deep and contain 1/7 of diameter.  There is broadening at the last whorl.The width of the shell is 4.5–6 mm. The height of the shell is 1.7–3 mm.


== Ecology ==
Zonitoides arboreus is common in woodlands and humid habitats, also in gardens. In Europe it is found in greenhouses, and is occasionally reported outside as an escape.Snails in this species create and use love darts.
Parasites of Zonitoides arboreus include:

Parelaphostrongylus tenuis
