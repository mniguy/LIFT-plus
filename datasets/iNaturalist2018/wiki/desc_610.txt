Cnaphalocrocis poeyalis, the lesser rice-leafroller, is a species of moth of the family Crambidae described by Jean Baptiste Boisduval in 1833. They can be found from Africa to the Pacific region, including Australia, RÃ©union, India, Fiji, Hong Kong and French Polynesia.
Their wingspan is about 20 mm.


== Nutrition ==
They feed on grasses (Poaceae) and are a pest of rice (Oryza sativa).
