Commelina dianthifolia, known as the birdbill dayflower, is a perennial herb native to the south-western United States (Arizona, Colorado, New Mexico, Texas) and northern Mexico. Petals are blue while sepals are green. The inflorescence is a scorpioid cyme and it is subtended by a boat-like spathe.


== Uses ==
An infusion of plant used by Keres people as a strengthener for weakened tuberculosis patients.
The Ramah Navajo give a cold simple or compound infusion to livestock as an aphrodisiac.
