Himantoglossum robertianum  is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to the Mediterranean Basin.


== Description ==
Himantoglossum robertianum is a bulbous plant. It flowers from January to April. The bulb is edible when cooked.


== Distribution and habitat ==
Himantoglossum robertianum is native to the Mediterranean Basin and is found in Portugal, Morocco, Spain, Balearic Islands, France, Italy, Sardegna, Corsica, Algeria, Libya, Yugoslavia, Greece, Aegean Islands and Crete, Anatolia and Cyprus. It is found in short, poor grassland, garrigue, scrub, and open woodland. It prefers dry to moist, alkaline and calcareous substrates. It can be found up to 1,700 m (5,600 ft) altitude.
